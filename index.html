Primary concern areas: Model Risk Data Exposure Prompt Injection Agent Tool Permissions Third-Party Model Dependency

Risk function assessment: The volume of AI use case submissions has increased 40% quarter-over-quarter, outpacing the current review capacity of the AI governance team. Seven submitted use cases involve third-party large language models with access to sensitive data classes, raising concerns under PIPEDA and data classification expectations; three have been conditionally approved pending data residency confirmation from the vendor. Two use cases involving automated underwriting decision support have been escalated to the Model Risk Committee for independent review, as outputs may be relied upon in a manner that requires model validation under OSFI Guideline E-23. Runtime monitoring of approved generative AI use cases identified four policy violations this quarter — primarily attempted prompt-injection and data-handling deviations — all contained without data exposure; agent tool permissions are being narrowed in response. The Technology Risk function has recommended a temporary intake freeze on Tier 1 AI use cases until a formal AI risk framework, currently in draft with control mapping to NIST AI RMF and ISO/IEC 42001, receives sign-off from the Chief Risk Officer and is socialized with the Audit Committee. An AI-specific incident response annex covering model rollback, output containment, and vendor notification is in draft alongside the framework.

What this demonstrates: AI intake governance, model oversight, regulatory alignment, runtime monitoring, and emerging-risk assessment.
